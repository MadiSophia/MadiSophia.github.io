About

Hiya:) My name is Madi, I'm a multi-disciplinary geospatial scientist currently located on the unseeded ʷməθkʷəy̓əm (Musqueam), Sḵwx̱wú7mesh (Squamish), and Səlilwətaɬ (Tsleil-Waututh) territory (Vancouver, BC). I grew up in Perth, Australia, and Bologna, Italy, but spent most of my childhood in Bend, OR, USA.

My love for and curiosity about the landscapes around me pushed me to study a BSc in Soil Science and Geology. During my studies, I discovered remote sensing and geospatial sciences and was immediately inspired by their powerful applications. My goal is to use remote sensing and other forms of spatial data to develop solutions to real-world problems.

Currently, I am an MSc student in the Integrated Remote Sensing studio at the University of British Columbia, supervised by Dr. Nicholas Coops. My research focuses on detecting and structurally characterizing non-stand replacing disturbances (such as insect infestations, landslides, surface fires, etc.) using a combination of moderate resolution satellite data (Landsat and Sentinel-2) and LiDAR data.

I am a competent geospatial programmer with experience working on various projects. These include assessing the vulnerability of glacier retreat in BC and Alaska, managing and developing spatial databases for a copper exploration project, and my current research on detecting and assessing the impact of forest disturbances. I am also passionate about science communication. For the past two years, I have been a teaching assistant for an introductory conservation course. Additionally, I have experience developing marketing and educational materials for a small urban farm.

Outside of work, I love to ride (and occasionally race) all kinds of bikes, ski, trail run, explore new places, drink tea, and do arts + crafts projects.

I can work in the US, Canada, and hopefully soon in the EU (I am in the process of obtaining my Italian citizenship).
